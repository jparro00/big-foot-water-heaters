FAQ

The questions everyone asks.

Don't see yours? Call (555) 123-4567.

How long does a water heater last?

Standard tanks: 8–12 years. Tankless: 15–20 years. Heat pump: 10–15 years. If yours is past those numbers, you're on borrowed time — sediment buildup, anode-rod failure, and tank corrosion are the usual culprits.

Tank or tankless — which should I get?

Tank is cheaper up front and works well for 1–4 person homes. Tankless costs more to install but lasts twice as long, never runs out of hot water, and lowers your gas bill. For families of 4+, or anyone planning to stay in the house 10+ years, tankless usually wins on lifetime cost.

Do you pull the permit?

Yes — every install. It's included in the flat rate. Skipping the permit is how unlicensed installers undercut us; it also voids your homeowner's insurance if anything ever goes wrong.

What about my old water heater?

We haul it. It leaves with us when we leave. No extra fee.

Why is your price lower than the chains?

Two reasons. First, we don't run a call center, a fleet of salespeople, or a national TV ad budget — that overhead is baked into chain pricing. Second, water heaters are all we do, so we're faster on every job and our trucks are stocked, which cuts time-on-site dramatically.

Do you offer financing?

What if my install needs something extra?

If we find something the listed price doesn't cover — say, the gas line needs to be resized for a tankless, or the venting is out of code — we'll quote it on the spot, in writing, before we touch it. You decide whether to proceed.
